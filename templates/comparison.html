Who is The GOAT

In the realm of football, debates over who truly deserves the title of the Greatest of All Time (GOAT) often ignite fiery discussions among fans worldwide. While acknowledging the exceptional talents of numerous players, the debate invariably narrows down to select individuals who have left an indelible mark on the sport. Among these luminaries stand two towering figures: Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo. Both players boast unparalleled achievements and have redefined the game in their own unique ways. However, as the adage goes, there can only be one GOAT which we decided to be Lionel Messi. So, let's delve deeper into the illustrious careers of these footballing icons to unravel the essence of their greatness.

The numbers paint a clear picture: Ronaldo consistently outpaces Messi in shot attempts, tallying an impressive 3677 shots in league and Champions League matches since 2009/10, compared to Messi's 2972.
Yet, Messi's efficiency is a marvel. He converts a higher percentage of his shots, finding the back of the net with every 5.27 attempts, whereas Ronaldo scores with every 6.43 shots.

Messi's accuracy is another feather in his cap, with 47.21% of his shots on target compared to Ronaldo's 41.09%.
However, Ronaldo's ability to generate such a high volume of attempts is remarkable in itself. Additionally, his prowess in shooting from long-range, as well as with his weaker foot and head, levels the playing field.

In this statistical showdown, it's a tight race. While Messi boasts superior efficiency and accuracy, Ronaldo's sheer volume and versatility make this one too close to call.

Messi and Ronaldo continually astound with their remarkable prowess on the field, boasting an impressive combined total of 122 hat tricks. Messi has notched 57 career hat tricks, while Ronaldo has accumulated 65.

While Ronaldo holds the lead in total hat tricks, Messi edges ahead slightly in terms of frequency. Messi achieves a hat trick every 18.5 games, whereas Ronaldo achieves one every 18.7 games. Delving deeper into their statistics, in league competitions, Messi has secured 36 hat tricks compared to Ronaldo's 44. Similarly, in the Champions League, both Messi and Ronaldo have achieved 8 hat tricks each. Beyond these, their domination extends to other competitions, with Messi having scored 13 hat tricks in domestic cup competitions and Ronaldo boasting 13 as well.

Their extraordinary talent transcends borders, with Messi tallying 14 hat tricks for his national team, Argentina, while Ronaldo has recorded an astounding 9 hat tricks for Portugal. These numbers not only underscore their individual brilliance but also serve as a testament to their enduring impact on the world of football. While Messi may have the edge in hat-trick conversion, Ronaldo's consistency and frequency showcase his remarkable skill and impact on the game.

Ronaldo's ability from the penalty spot often overshadows Messi's abilities, but the gap between them might not be as wide as commonly believed. While Ronaldo is celebrated as the ultimate penalty king, the reality is more nuanced.

Excluding shootouts, Ronaldo has successfully converted an impressive 164 penalties throughout his career, but he's also missed 29. In contrast, Messi has netted 109 penalties but has missed 31. This gives Ronaldo an overall penalty conversion rate of 85%, compared to Messi's 78%. While Ronaldo's numbers are indeed better, he's not infallible, especially when compared to other elite strikers like Lewandowski and Ibrahimovic, who boast even higher conversion rates.

In terms of notable misses, Messi stumbled in the 2016 Copa America final shootout against Chile and the 2012 Champions League semi-final against Chelsea. Meanwhile, Ronaldo's significant misses include the heartbreak of the 2008 Champions League final shootout against Chelsea (although his team, Manchester United, ultimately prevailed) and the pivotal 2012 Champions League semi-final shootout against Bayern Munich, which proved costly for Real Madrid.

In recent times, Messi has undoubtedly elevated himself as the premier free kick specialist, while Ronaldo's proficiency in this domain has seen a noticeable decline. However, to label this category as a clear-cut victory for Messi would be succumbing to recency bias.

Consider this: from 2017 to 2019, Messi showcased his free kick mastery with an impressive 23 goals, leaving Ronaldo trailing with just 5. Yet, cast your mind back to the years spanning 2009 to 2011, and you'll find Ronaldo asserting his dominance with an astonishing 21 direct free kick goals compared to Messi's modest 3.
Looking at their overall career statistics, Messi now boasts 65 free kick goals, slightly surpassing Ronaldo's tally of 63.

While Ronaldo once seemed unassailable in this aspect, Messi's resurgence has not only closed the gap but also surpassed it. Furthermore, Messi now flaunts a superior free kick conversion rate, giving him the edge in this closely contested category.

When it comes to heading, there's no denying Ronaldo's dominance over Messi. The numbers speak volumes: throughout their careers, Ronaldo has soared with 148 headed goals in 1217 appearances, whereas Messi has managed just 27 headers in 1056 appearances.

But it's not just about goals. Ronaldo's aerial prowess extends beyond scoring, as evidenced by his commanding presence in winning 781 aerial duels in league and Champions League matches since 2009/10, while Messi has claimed victory in only 117 aerial duels.
It's not surprising when you consider their physical attributes, playing styles, and the tactics of their teams. Ronaldo's towering presence and athleticism naturally lend themselves to aerial battles, while Messi's smaller stature and nuanced approach see him excel in other facets of the game.

In this category, Ronaldo's superiority is crystal clear, underlining his unparalleled ability in the air.

When it comes to passing, Messi undeniably takes the spotlight, but Ronaldo's abilities in this area often fly under the radar.
In league and Champions League matches since 2009/10, Messi has showcased his playmaking prowess with a staggering 1406 key passes in 599 appearances, while Ronaldo has contributed 942 key passes in 561 appearances.

Messi's dominance extends to throughballs as well, with 450 successful throughballs in the same timeframe, compared to Ronaldo's 81. While Messi's passing statistics stand out as superior, Ronaldo's numbers are still noteworthy. It's worth considering that Ronaldo's involvement in creative build-up play may have been more pronounced before 2009, which could have significantly boosted his passing stats during that period.

In this category, Messi shines brightly, but Ronaldo's passing abilities should not be overlooked.

When it comes to assists, Messi stands tall as the undisputed champion. His playmaking abilities rank among the best, and the numbers speak for themselves. With an astounding 366 assists in 1056 career appearances, Messi's prowess in setting up goals is undeniable, dwarfing Ronaldo's respectable tally of 250 assists in 1217 games.

However, it's essential not to overlook Ronaldo's proficiency in this aspect. While Messi holds a clear advantage in overall assists, Ronaldo's numbers are still incredibly impressive compared to the average player.
In the prestigious arena of the Champions League, Ronaldo shines brightly with 41 assists to Messi's 40. Granted, Ronaldo has played 20 more matches than Messi, but this achievement is still noteworthy.

While Ronaldo's assist numbers may not reach the heights of Messi's, they underscore his versatility and impact on the game. Nonetheless, Messi's remarkable overall assist stats rightfully earn him the title of the ultimate playmaker.

The sustained excellence of Messi and Ronaldo is truly unparalleled in football history. Their dominance over the coveted Ballon d'Or award exemplifies this, with Messi securing a remarkable 8 trophies compared to Ronaldo's impressive haul of 5. It's worth noting that no other player in history has managed to win more than 3 Ballons d'Or. The rivalry between Messi and Ronaldo has undoubtedly fueled their remarkable achievements, and it's not unthinkable that either of them could have clinched the award even more times if not for each other's presence.

In UEFA's prestigious awards, Ronaldo holds a slight edge with 4 accolades, including 1 UEFA Club Footballer of the Year, 2 UEFA Best Player in Europe titles, and 1 UEFA Men's Player of the Year award, while Messi has claimed 3 awards, including 1 UEFA Club Footballer of the Year and 2 UEFA Best Player in Europe titles.
When it comes to individual scoring accolades, Messi has emerged as the top scorer in the league an astounding 8 times, clinching the European Golden Shoe on 6 occasions. Ronaldo, meanwhile, has claimed the league's top scorer title 5 times across various leagues, earning 4 European Golden Shoes in the process.

In terms of spectacular goals, Ronaldo has clinched the Puskas award for the best goal in a calendar year, securing the honor twice with 2 nominations. Messi, despite being nominated an impressive 7 times, has yet to claim this award.
On the international stage, Messi has showcased his brilliance by winning the Golden Ball twice at the World Cup, in 2014 and 2022, leading his team to the final in the former. Additionally, he has been named Copa America's Best Player twice and Best Young Player once, further solidifying his status as a football icon.

In the realm of trophies, Messi holds the overall lead, boasting an impressive tally of 44 trophies compared to Ronaldo's 35. This count includes Messi's triumphs in the 2008 Olympics and the 2005 U-20 World Cup.
On the international stage, Messi has recently added both the Copa America and the World Cup with Argentina to his illustrious resume, while Ronaldo has secured the Euros with Portugal as well as a Nations League trophy.

Delving into their club achievements, Messi's 12 league titles surpass Ronaldo's 7, yet Ronaldo claims the upper hand in Champions League triumphs, with 5 to Messi's 4. While the difference is marginal, Messi's overall trophy count currently places him ahead in this category.

It's fascinating to compare the international careers of Ronaldo and Messi, two giants of the modern football era. While their club performances often steal the spotlight, their contributions to their national teams are also significant.

Ronaldo's goal-scoring prowess for Portugal is truly remarkable, especially considering his status as the all-time leading goal scorer for his country. He has a knack for delivering when it matters most, evidenced by his Golden Boot at Euro 2021 and his success in major tournaments like the Euros and the UEFA Nations League.

On the other hand, Messi's impact for Argentina is undeniable, even if his goal-scoring rate is slightly lower than Ronaldo's. His ability to create and influence games is unparalleled, earning him numerous individual awards and, most importantly, leading Argentina to victory in the Copa America and the World Cup, fulfilling a lifelong dream and ending years of heartache for his nation.

While Ronaldo boasts an impressive collection of records and achievements at the international level, Messi's combination of individual accolades and team success, including triumphs in the most prestigious tournaments, sets him apart.
